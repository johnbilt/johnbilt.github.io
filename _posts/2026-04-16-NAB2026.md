With NAB 2026 rapidly approaching we've put together a list of some of the places where you can see or talk about the latest developments in the TAMS eco system.

As with previous trade shows, the main multi-vendor demonstration will be on the AWS booth.  However with more and more products supporting TAMS then we wanted to provide a simple guide on where to find TAMS around the show.

## AWS (W1701)

The AWS booth will once again host a multi-partner TAMS demo bringing together many of the latest developments in the TAMS eco-system in one place.  The demo will include TAMS native camera to cloud, integrations between TAMS and the EBU Media Exchange Layer (MXL), new and updated editing capabilities, compliance recording capabilities plus generative AI for near live video understanding and agentic workflows.

![Diagram of the TAMS demo at the AWS Booth](/images/blog_images/2026-nab-demo-diagram.png)

## CuttingRoom (N2172)

Edit directly from your TAMS store in the CuttingRoom Cloud Editor. Add growing TAMS recordings even directly to your timeline.  Shoot directly to your TAMS store with the mobile news gathering app CuttingRoom Reporter, enabling editing while shooting. 

[https://www.cuttingroom.com](https://www.cuttingroom.com)

## Konstrukt

Konstrukt’s Compliance Vault gives media companies a frame‑accurate, reliable, and readily available record of everything that goes to air or online, along with the ability to act on it quickly. Built as a cloud‑native workflow on TAMS, Compliance Vault lets media teams record once and reuse the same time‑indexed media across applications well beyond compliance. The record‑once model, combined with a serverless architecture, makes the solution’s economics attractive and cost‑optimized. Using Omakase Player (player.byomakase.org), the native TAMS player included in the open‑source TAMS reference implementation, Compliance Vault provides teams with a browser‑based application to efficiently review, edit, and export media.

[https://bykonstrukt.com](https://bykonstrukt.com) | [https://player.byomakase.org](https://player.byomakase.org)

## LOGIC media solutions GmbH

LOGIC’s modular platform demonstrates how we build custom, cloud-native media workflows based on TAMS. Using a soccer highlight use case as a reference implementation, it shows how modules such as ingest, playback, edit-by-reference, event-driven highlight processing, and AI can be combined into tailored workflows for specific workflows and requirements rather than one-size-fits-all systems. The project is shortlisted for the IABM Impact Awards 2026 in the Transformational Project Award category.

 [https://logic.tv/](https://logic.tv/)

## LucidLink (N2940)

LucidLink Connect revolutionises the creative workflow by seamlessly integrating TAMs Store with any NLE. Leveraging advanced file-streaming technology, it enables editors to access and edit growing files in real time, eliminating the need for local downloads or cumbersome transfers.

[https://www.lucidlink.com/events/nab-2026](https://www.lucidlink.com/events/nab-2026)

## Matrox (N2451)

Matrox ORIGIN is SDK that allows developers to build media application using simple REST APIs.  It provides a complete suite of media processing functions including TAMS readers and writers.  In the demo, ORIGIN plays back flows from TAMS into ORIGIN Fabric which are also available in MXL, at the same time, ORIGIN/MXL flows are written into TAMS

[https://video.matrox.com/en/products/broadcast-media/origin-series](https://video.matrox.com/en/products/broadcast-media/origin-series)


## Mavis (N2161)

Mavis Camera is designed to connect a wide range of acquisition sources into modern production workflows. In this demo, we extend our existing camera-to-cloud workflows by supporting TAMS from the internal iPhone camera and external HDMI and SDI sources through devices such as Atomos Ninja Phone and the Accsoon SeeMo range. This shows how Mavis can bridge traditional video hardware and cloud-native production, bringing signals from professional cameras and other external gear into the same real-time workflow.  

[https://mavis.cloud](https://mavis.cloud)

## Mimir (N2850)

Mimir is a cloud-native platform that simplifies how creative teams manage their media. It acts as a unified "brain" for your assets, allowing editors to find and use files instantly without worrying about where they are physically stored. Whether your high-res footage is on a local server, a NAS, or in the cloud (like AWS S3 or TAMS), Mimir handles the technical logistics so you can stay focused on the storytelling.

[https://onemimir.com/news/see-mimir-at-nab-2026](https://onemimir.com/news/see-mimir-at-nab-2026)

## Norsk (W3113)

Norsk's TAMS (Time-Addressable Media Store) integration enables live content to become immediately queryable as it's captured, eliminating the traditional boundary between live production and VOD workflows. TAMS, an open-source specification developed by BBC R&D and now adopted by AWS, Adobe, Sky, and others, stores segmented media in cloud object storage with HTTP API access indexed by timeline position. Norsk's MediaStore component brings this capability natively into Norsk Studio workflows.

[https://norsk.video](https://norsk.video)


## Reuters Imagen (W1624)

Reuters Imagen provides real-time activation of live content through multi‑cloud TAMS ingest. Automated enrichment against the content in TAMS ensures live assets are immediately discoverable, enriched, and available for downstream automation and use.

[https://imagen.io](https://imagen.io)

## Techex (W2267)

tx darwrin provides comprehensive manipulation and syncrhonisation of transport streams ahead of writing to a TAMS store. tx darwin is a software platform which runs in the cloud or on-prem supporting compressed or uncompressed media workflows. Now, it's possible to synchronise miltiple feeds on writing, write as multiple essences or a single combined stream and even perform video.and audio confromance ahead of a TAMS media store. 

[https://techex.tv](https://imagen.io)

## Zixi (W2057)

Zixi enables broadcasters to bridge live streaming workflows with TAMS-based VOD environments. At NAB, Zixi will demonstrate how live streams can be ingested and written as assets into a TAMS store, making live content immediately available for downstream clipping, archive, compliance, and monetization workflows. ZEN Master provides orchestration, monitoring, and per-stream or per-event control for flexible and reliable operations

[https://zixi.com](https://zixi.com)
